Situation where I had an ethical decision to make

A couple of years ago, when I was a head chef of a bar, one of my staff members started to have problems and arguments with the rest of the staff.
The situation was getting worse and worse every day, to the point where this person started to show up late and didn't have the best performance during their shifts.
At this point I had plenty of evidence to be able to dismiss this person - but after meeting with them about it, I realized that they were having serious problems with alcohol.
I brought up this issue with our boss, and we decided that instead of dismissing them we were going to help them to find mental help with a counsellor, and encourage them to attend AA meetings.

How my culture has influenced my values and identity

Something good that I think I take from my culture is the value of working hard when I get committed to a project, and not giving up until it is completed.
Alongside this, I always like working in a team environment where everybody is working together to reach the same goal.

Strengths that might help me in my learning journey

One of the strengths that I'm most proud of is my determination to accomplish my commitments and that I hardly ever give up on things, no matter how hard the challenge is.

My experience when I tried to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension

When I first got the promotion to the head chef position I was underestimated by my staff members, so it was pretty hard for me to be taken seriously at the beginning.
After a period of hard work guiding by example, and always working as a team, I was able to break through that tension.
Everything was much easier from that point, and people respected me much more.

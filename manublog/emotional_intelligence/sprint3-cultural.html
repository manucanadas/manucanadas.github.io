Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

It's the ability that people have to understand their own emotions, and the emotions of other people - and the skill of managing those emotions in positive ways.
This is not an ability that we are born with, it's an ability we have to develop.

What is IQ?

Intelligence quotient (IQ), is a measure of a person's reasoning ability.
It is a way to gauge how well someone can use information and logic to solve problems.

What is the difference between EQ and IQ?

The difference between IQ and EQ is that while IQ is the measure of a person's relative intelligence and the ability to use logic to solve problems.
EQ is the ability to identify and manage your emotions and other people's emotions, considering other people's opinions to solve a problem in the best way possible.

Why is EQ so important?

We can't say that EQ is more important that IQ, both of them are equal parts of our intelligence.
What we can say is as children we have been educated in a way that cares more about developing our IQ than our EQ (the school system).
But it's important to develop our EQ as well, so we can improve our skill of looking at a problem from different points of view and trying to find the best solution.
Developing our EQ will give us the chance to improve our performance, leadership skills and happiness.

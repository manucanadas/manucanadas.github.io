Reflections

Three new thing I've learned about myself and my ego

One of the most important things that I learned is the idea of developing my EQ, or the idea of growth mindset.
Coming from a fixed academy backround, the idea of learnig new skills and learning how to learn them fascinates me and gives me the motivation not to give up.
Talking about my ego, I would say that I'm developing the skill of asking, I'm learning there is no shame in asking even the most dumb questions,
Sometimes asking a simple question at the right moment can save us a lot of time, and also that question can help others with the same or a similar doubt.
I've also improve my time managing skills, this is something that I would like to keep improving.

The role of values, empathy and self awarness in learning and programming

I believe that the role of these people-skills are essential in the programming learning process,
Knowing that most of the time we are going to be working in team environments, developing these skills will help us when we have to interact with others.
listening to others' ideas, putting yourself in others' boots and having the empathy of understanding others are crucial skills that employers are looking for.

What has surprised me the most about the core learning?

What surprised me the most was the constant changes in the learning proccess methods,
The idea of learning "how to learn" and the different ways and methods we have to develop our learning process is a new concept that I would like to embrace.

What are the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

The most challenging aspects of the core learning was and still is the developing of time management skills.

Why is a programming school spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

I believe that EDA spend so much time in developing these non-technical skills because they know that employers don't look just for people with good technical skills.
They also look for people with great people-skills, people with the ability to understand their own emotions, manage them and use them in a positive way.

Is spending time in studying core learning a waste of time?

Honestly at the beginning of the course it was exactly what I thought, I didn't understand why we were spending so much time in this aspect,
but when I learned and understood a bit more about it, I completely changed my mind.

My long term goal and/or career pathway

My long term goal is to be able to grow professionaly in the programming world.
Been a foreign person I would love to have the possibility of finding a stable job with the chance of travelling and working remotely.

What do I think my biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

I believe that my biggest strengths will be my capacity to work in a team, I have always loved to do team work, having the possibility of taking different roles in the group,
and also learning from the other group members.
I think that one of my main limitations will be the language barrier, now that we've finished foundations I realize that it was something that I didn't think was going to be a problem.
I can say now that it is, I've noticed that it took me a lot of extra time to understand what the challenges were asking me to do.

What do I think my biggest non-technical challenge will be?

Apart from the understanding of the english language, that is an everyday learning journey, I would like to improve the skill of recognising when is time to ask for help.
I feel like some times I waste too much time stuck in a situation, that it could be easily solved by asking for help in the right moment.

What non-technical skills would I like to see developed in myself while at EDA?

I would like to develop the skill of recognising the right moment to ask for help.

What are my expectations of myself on Bootcamp?

I have high expectations of myself, coming back to school after so many years, and diving into a completely new world for me is super exciting.
I expect that every day there is going to be something new to learn, and I hope that I can be kind and patient with myself when I get stuck.
Knowing that I'm going through a new and difficult experience full of big challenges, but every single one of them come with a bigger reword once we solve them.
I'm looking forward to meeting new people to transit this path with. And last but not lest, I EXPECT TO HAVE FUN!!!

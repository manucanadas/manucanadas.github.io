Problem Solving

What was the problem?

I had problems with pretty much every single exercise of this sprint, I found myself getting stuck very often and getting very frustrated.
It was during the FizzBuzz exercise that I first realized that I wasn't understanding what I had to do.
I found myself looking at the tasks and not even knowing where to start. I think that a main problem I have is that I need to pay attention when I am reading, as I find myself having to read things multiple times before I feel like I understand.

What problem solving techniques did I use to help solve the problem?

After reading the assignment multiple times and still feeling stuck, I used the rubber ducky method, and read the assignment out loud to my thermos.
Once I did this, I began with the pseudocode technique, trying to build small pieces of my code and using the technique of googling extra information to help me with this part.

How did I feel throughout this process?

I honestly felt super stressed out, like I wasn't understanding the subject of the assignment.
To an extent I still feel this way but I did make some progress as well.

What did I learn?

What I have learnt is that I am wasting far too much time trying to solve these problems by myself.
Reaching out to my cohort or to my facilitators more often would help me learn things much quicker, and that is what I should be doing more often.
I just need to be confident to do that, because there is no shame in reaching out for help.
